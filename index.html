Explore Cox,s Bazar

Cox’s Bazar, located in southeastern Bangladesh, is famous for having the longest natural sea beach in the world — stretching about 120 kilometers along the Bay of Bengal. It’s one of the most popular tourist destinations in Bangladesh, known for its golden sandy beaches, rolling waves, and breathtaking sunsets.

Saintmartin

Saint Martin Bangladesh, in the Chittagong Division, is the only coral island of the country. The local people call this tiny island “NarikelZinjira” meaning coconut island in their native language because of the abundant coconut growing here. The island is located in the southernmost part of mainland just at the river mouth of Naf river..

Moheskhali

The government's ambitious project to transform Maheshkhali Island in the Bay of Bengal into a thriving energy hub aims to establish power plants and a liquefied natural gas (LNG) terminal by 2027. This endeavor is driven by the objective of strengthening the country's economy and achieving self-reliance in the power and fuel sector.

Marine Drive Road

A serene stretch of the Marine Drive in Cox’s Bazar, one of Bangladesh’s most beautiful coastal highways. The road, which stretches 80 km from Cox’s Bazar to Teknaf, provides stunning views of the Bay of Bengal and verdant hills on opposite sides. Marine Drive is well known for its breathtaking natural beauty, including tranquil beaches, perfect locations for watching sunsets.

Dried Fish Corner

According to local traders, the best place is Nazirartek located in Ward No. 1 of Cox's Bazar Municipality. Known as the largest dry fish center in South Asia, this area has numerous small shops selling a wide range of locally processed dry fish at prices much lower than other parts of the city. Thousands of tourists come here and purchase dry fish.
